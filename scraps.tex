Now let us consider the other commuting diagram
\bcd
\mathcal{L}(X,A,B) \ar[r,"\ev \times \pi"] \ar[rr, bend right = 20, "\ev"] & X^r \times X^r \times \MM_{0,A,B}^{\operatorname{wt}} \ar[r,"\pi_1"] & X^r \times X^r
\ecd
which produces an exact triangle:
\begin{equation*} \pi^* L_{\MM_{0,A,B}^{\operatorname{wt}}} \to L_{\ev} \to L_{\ev \times \pi} \xrightarrow{[1]} \end{equation*}
By composing $E_{\ev \times \pi} \to L_{\ev \times \pi}$ with the connecting homomorphism we obtain a morphism of exact triangles:
\bcd
\pi^* L_{\MM_{0,A,B}^{\operatorname{wt}}} \ar[r] \ar[d,"\Id"] & E_{\ev} \ar[r] \ar[d] & E_{\ev \times \pi} \ar[r,"{[1]}"] \ar[d] & \, \\
\pi^* L_{\MM_{0,A,B}^{\operatorname{wt}}} \ar[r] & L_{\ev} \ar[r] & L_{\ev \times \pi} \ar[r,"{[1]}"] & \,
\ecd
We claim that $E_{\ev} \to L_{\ev}$ is a relative perfect obstruction theory. Since $\pi^* L_{\MM_{0,A,B}^{\operatorname{wt}}}$ and $E_{\ev \times \pi}$ are concentrated in degrees $[0,1]$ and $[-1,0]$ respectively, it follows that $E_{\ev}$ is concentrated in degrees $[-1,1]$. In fact we claim that $\h^{1}(E_{\ev})=0$. Consider a piece of the long exact cohomology sequence:
\begin{equation*} \h^0(E_{\ev \times \pi}) \to \h^1(\pi^* L_{\MM_{0,A,B}^{\operatorname{wt}}}) \to \h^1 (E_{\ev}) \to 0 \end{equation*}
Thus we have to show that the first map is surjectie. Consider the morphism of exact triangles:
\bcd
\ev^* L_{X^r \times X^r} \ar[r] \ar[d] & E_\pi \ar[r] \ar[d] & E_{\ev \times \pi} \ar[d] \ar[r,"{[1]}"] & \, \\
0 \ar[r] & \pi^* L_{\MM_{0,A,B}^{\operatorname{wt}}}[1] \ar[r,"\Id"] & \pi^* L_{\MM_{0,A,B}^{\operatorname{wt}}}[1] \ar[r,"{[1]}"] & \,
\ecd
Passing to cohomology we have
\bcd
\h^0(E_\pi) \ar[r] \ar[d] & \h^0(E_{\ev \times \pi}) \ar[r] \ar[d] & 0 \ar[r] \ar[d] & 0 \ar[d] \\
\h^1(\pi^* L_{\MM_{0,A,B}^{\operatorname{wt}}}) \ar[r] & \h^1(\pi^* L_{\MM_{0,A,B}^{\operatorname{wt}}}) \ar[r] & 0 \ar[r] & 0
\ecd
and so by the 4--lemma the second vertical map is surjective if the first is. But this morphism is induced by the exact triangle
\begin{equation*} \pi^* L_{\MM_{0,A,B}^{\operatorname{wt}}} \to E \to E_\pi \xrightarrow{[1]} \end{equation*}
(where $E$ is the total obstruction theory), from which we obtain
\begin{equation*} \h^0(E_\pi) \to \h^1(\pi^* L_{\MM_{0,A,B}^{\operatorname{wt}}}) \to 0 \end{equation*}
because $E$ is concentrated in degrees $[-1,0]$. So $\h^1(E_{\ev})=0$ as claimed. It is then a straightforward application of the $5$--lemma to show that the morphism $E_{\ev} \to L_{\ev}$ satisfies the required properties on $\h^{-1}$ and $\h^0$.

Thus, we have a perfect obstruction theory $E_{\ev} \to L_{\ev}$ for $\mathcal{L}(X,A,B)$ over $X^r \times X^r$ which, by the nature of its construction, induces the standard virtual class. 

Then by compatibility of the virtual class with pullback (\cite[Proposition 7.2]{BF}) we have that
\begin{equation*} h^* E_{\ev} \to h^* L_{\ev} = L_{\ev} \end{equation*}
is a perfect obstruction theory on $\mathcal{K}(X,A,B)$ over $X^r$ which induces the virtual fundamental class $\virt{\mathcal{K}(X,A,B)}$ as defined earlier.
